## Instructions

If "Skipped" above is "true", the candidate did not answer this question. Return all fields as null — do not score or analyze a skipped question.

If "Skipped" is "false", evaluate the answer using the four dimensions below. Score each dimension as an integer from 1 to 10.

### Scoring Dimensions

**relevance** — Does the answer directly address the question asked? A score of 10 means the answer stays on-topic and fully responds to what was asked. A score of 1 means the answer is entirely off-topic or does not respond to the question at all.

**specificity** — How well does the answer follow the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result)? Score based on how many elements are clearly present and grounded in a real experience:
- 1–3: Only one or no STAR elements present; answer is vague or generic
- 4–6: Two or three STAR elements present but incomplete or ungrounded
- 7–9: All four elements present; Action and Result are concrete
- 10: All four elements are vivid, specific, and tied to measurable or observable outcomes

**structure** — How logically organized and coherent is the answer? Does it flow from context → action → outcome? Does the listener always know where the story is going?

**communication** — How clear, concise, and confident is the delivery? Penalize rambling, filler-heavy responses, excessive hedging, or answers that are so terse the listener cannot follow.

### Text Fields

**strongest_moment** — Identify the single best thing the candidate said. Quote or closely paraphrase something they **actually said** — do not write a generic observation like "they demonstrated leadership." If nothing stands out, write null.

**weakest_moment** — Identify the biggest gap or missed opportunity. Quote or closely paraphrase something they **actually said** (or a specific omission traceable to their words) — do not write a generic observation like "they lacked specifics." If there is no clear weakness, write null.

**suggested_rewrite** — Write one improved version of the weakest moment as natural speech the candidate could have said. It must sound like a person speaking, not a coaching note. Do NOT use phrases like "You should have said" or "A better answer would be." Start directly with the improved statement. If weakest_moment is null, this must also be null.

### Hard Rules

- Do NOT reference the scoring rubric, dimensions, or STAR in the suggested_rewrite — it must read as natural speech.
- strongest_moment and weakest_moment must reference something specific from the transcript, not generic coaching language.
- Return exactly the JSON structure below. No extra fields. No explanation outside the JSON.
